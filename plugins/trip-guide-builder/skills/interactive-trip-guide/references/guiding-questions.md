# Guiding questions

Ask these up front, in a single batch, before building. The goal is to gather everything the
itinerary depends on without a long back-and-forth. Pre-fill anything you can already infer
from the uploaded tickets or hotel (dates, destination, number of travelers) and just ask the
user to confirm, so you're not asking what you already know.

## Essential (the plan can't be right without these)

1. **Destination(s) & base(s)** — which city or cities. **List every hotel/accommodation with
   the nights each one covers** — a trip may have a single base or several (multi-city, or a
   hotel change mid-trip). Each night's hotel is that day's anchor. If they gave a hotel list,
   map each hotel to its date range and confirm. Flag any **changeover day** (check out of one
   hotel and into the next) — those days need checkout + luggage + transfer built in.
2. **Exact dates** — arrival and departure. Cross-check against the flight tickets. Work out
   the day of week for each date (drives venue closed-days and the arrival/departure logic).
3. **Travelers** — how many adults and how many children, and **each child's age**. Ages
   change everything (toddler vs. 8-year-old vs. teen): playground vs. museum, free admission
   thresholds, walking tolerance, nap/rest needs, restaurant play-areas.
4. **Home currency & language** — for the budget's multi-currency display and whether the
   guide is RTL (Hebrew) or LTR.

## Shaping (defaults are fine if they don't answer)

5. **Interests / must-dos** — e.g. nature & parks, science/interactive museums, animals,
   history, food, shopping, water/beach. Also anything to avoid.
6. **Pace** — packed vs. relaxed; how many hours/day the kids can handle; whether they want a
   midday rest built in.
7. **Budget style** — budget / mid-range / comfortable. Sets restaurant price tiers and the
   shopping allowance in the calculator.
8. **Transport preference** — public transport (cheapest, kids often free) vs. taxi/rideshare
   (easiest with young kids and luggage) vs. mix.
9. **Dietary / accessibility** — kosher/halal/vegetarian/allergies; stroller or wheelchair
   needs (affects venue and transit choices).
10. **Special occasions** — a birthday or anything to feature (e.g. a "grand finale" dinner).
11. **Between-city / between-hotel transfers** (multi-base trips only) — how they move between
    cities or hotels (booked train / flight / car, and on which dates), so each changeover day
    includes the right transfer logistics and timing.

## Derived from the tickets (don't ask — extract, then confirm)

- Flight numbers, times, airports/terminals, operating carrier, seats per passenger,
  baggage, booking reference (PNR).
- Arrival time → how late the first day realistically starts.
- Departure time → the departure-day cutoff (be at the airport ~3h before international).

If the user is vague, proceed with sensible defaults and say which defaults you chose, rather
than blocking on a perfect answer.
